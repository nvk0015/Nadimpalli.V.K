About Me

Growing up in a small town in a developing nation, my school days were a whirlwind of activities. From attending classes from 9:00 AM to 4:00 PM to engaging in field hockey matches after school until 6:00 PM, I always found myself balancing the demands of academic pursuits and extracurricular activities. Rushing back home for dinner with family, quickly finishing my homework, and preparing for bed marked the end of my weekday routine. On weekends, cricket matches and quality time with family and friends filled my days, providing a much-needed respite from the demands of school life. During my 11th standard, a subject called statistics and probability ignited a deep fascination within me. The concepts of descriptive statistics and the probability of selecting cards from a deck sparked a curiosity that extended beyond the classroom. For a couple of years, I found myself intuitively applying probabilistic thinking to various aspects of life. My fascination with machines and their ability to simplify and automate tasks grew stronger during my holidays on our family's rice farm. Witnessing the impact of machinery on reducing the burden of manual labor instilled in me a profound respect for engineering. This experience, coupled with my academic inclinations, led me to pursue mechanical engineering as my undergraduate specialization. This brief glimpse into my upbringing and educational journey highlights my passion for learning, my adaptability to diverse environments, and my inherent curiosity about the world around me. These qualities, I believe, will serve as the foundation for my future endeavors in mechanical engineering.
